# Contributing to QArcade

Thank you for helping build QArcade. This repository uses directory-level
license scopes. Before contributing, read `LICENSE`, `NOTICE`, and the nearest
`LICENSE_SCOPE.md` for every path you plan to change.

## General expectations

- Submit only work you created or have the right to contribute.
- Identify copied, adapted, generated, or externally sourced material.
- Preserve copyright, license, and attribution notices.
- Do not add proprietary SDKs, fonts, game exports, media, or datasets without
  documented redistribution permission.
- Keep code, educational content, hardware, and brand assets in their stated
  scopes. A contribution to one category does not grant rights in another.
- Describe significant use of generative AI and confirm that the contribution
  has been reviewed for provenance, quality, and third-party material.

By intentionally submitting a code contribution to a path covered by
`LICENSE-CODE`, you agree that the contribution is submitted under Apache-2.0,
as described in section 5 of that license, unless a written agreement says
otherwise. You retain copyright in your contribution.

## Code contributions

Code contributions are currently welcomed only in paths expressly covered by
`LICENSE-CODE`. Include tests when practical and follow directory-specific
development instructions. Do not combine game-owned missions, levels, art, or
curriculum with framework code unless the scope has been approved first.

Third-party code must include its exact source, version or commit, license,
copyright notices, and modification history.

## Content contributions

Content contributions are not automatically accepted under Apache-2.0. Before
submitting curriculum, prose, levels, missions, art, audio, or other creative
material, obtain maintainer agreement on the intended content license and
record every contributor and external source.

Until a content contribution is explicitly accepted under written terms, do
not assume that opening an issue or pull request changes its license status.

## Hardware contributions

Discuss hardware contributions before submitting CAD, STL, PCB, Gerber, BOM,
enclosure, wiring, or manufacturing files. State the intended hardware
license, design ownership, tool/export format, safety assumptions, and any
third-party components or reference designs.

No contribution should claim regulatory approval or imply that a derivative
is an official QArcade commercial product.

## Games and historical material

Existing game directories are outside the new root Apache-2.0 scope unless a
file-specific license says otherwise. Fixes to those directories require
separate confirmation from the relevant rights holders. Do not copy their
content into the open framework merely to change its license scope.

## Review checklist

A contribution should explain:

1. what changed and why;
2. which license scope applies;
3. whether third-party or AI-assisted material is present;
4. how the change was tested or reviewed; and
5. whether attribution or `NOTICE` must be updated.

The project may introduce a contributor license agreement or developer
certificate of origin after legal review. This draft does not create one.
